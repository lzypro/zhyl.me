Simplifying complexity has always been at the forefront of SmartX’s product design philosophy. Their constant investment in design makes the product experience much better than their competitors. However, it’s never easy to help potential users experience their products. Previously they’ve tried blog posts and instructional videos.

The problem with reading articles is even the most detailed one can hardly enable users to experience the actual hands-on feeling. And the cost of producing high quality video is just too high. For this reason, SmartX team developed a web-based screen recording tool that generates interactive programs with step-by-step prompts. I designed the experience of the content generated by the tool in collaboration with the product team. We have also worked together to design a community page that is closely related to the Product Tour.

Process

Goal

Less burden to start with.

In the field of IT infrastructure, a complex trial process not only affects perceptions of products — it also creates a heavy reliance on salespeople. No matter ordinary users, engineers, maintainers or resellers, they all want to try the products in a simple and clear way.

Initial Goals

During the kick-off phase, the product manager defined the following goals in the document:

  1. Provides demonstrations of installation, deployment and frequently used features of CloudTower.
  2. Anyone can view the tutorials without signing in.
  3. The tutorial is divided into multiple scenarios and tasks. Each task adopts an instructional approach. Once every task is finished, the user is guided to continue next one.
  4. Records user cookies in the browser and saves the progress of each scenario and task. After leaving the browser, the user can pick up the previous step when returning.
Original Prototype

Develop the concept and basic flow.

When developing the concept I tried 2 different directions. One of them is to provide a similar feel to video streaming service like Netlix, through a strongly immersive interface. The other one is to provide more information by textual explanations to help users understand the product inside and out.

After discussions with product managers and the CEO, we went with first direction and moved forward with the design.

Community

Flow

A collection of touch points and resources.

Considering the habits and preferences of Chinese users, SmartX did not plan to build a community as an open public space in the beginning. Instead, our expectation was to start with small group chats and engage users with rich community resources. Thus the challenge lies in how to organize the multiple forms of content and the relationship between each of them.
